## Player Characters

Each pilot is designed with an **intended starting identity**, including a natural build direction, ship profile, dialogue tone, and stat bias. These are not hard restrictions. Players should be able to bend, hybridize, or completely subvert intended roles through ship unlocks, upgrade choices, and faction adaptation.

Base pilot stats are currently framed around three identity attributes:

- **Perception** — awareness, targeting instinct, timing, precision, battlefield reading  
- **Charisma** — composure, presence, morale, instinctive control under pressure, influence-oriented effects  
- **Ingenuity** — systems thinking, improvisation, engineering, mechanical exploitation  

Stat values are currently directional and serve as long-term design anchors rather than fixed mechanics.

### Trey

**Role Identity:** Defensive bruiser, controlled pressure, durable escalation  
**Default Ship:** Heavy Bruiser  
**Build Direction:** High survivability with heavy ordnance and battlefield denial  

Trey begins with the most stable defensive baseline among early pilots. His intended build emphasizes slower positioning, deliberate weapon commitment, and durable scaling through hull, armor, and area denial.

His natural offense leans toward:
- missiles  
- rockets  
- mines  
- slower heavy-impact weapon systems  

He is less mobile than lighter pilots but rewards patient positioning and strategic upgrade choices that allow him to survive mistakes longer than others.

**Strengths**
- Strong defensive baseline  
- Reliable sustained pressure  
- High forgiveness under heavy wave pressure  

**Weaknesses**
- Lower mobility  
- Slower repositioning  
- Vulnerable to being overwhelmed if surrounded early  

**Starting Stats**
- **Perception:** Medium  
- **Charisma:** Low–Medium  
- **Ingenuity:** High

### Laura

**Role Identity:** Balanced vanilla pilot, adaptable scaling, clean fundamentals  
**Default Ship:** Light Fighter  
**Build Direction:** Fast, readable hybrid offense with strong upgrade flexibility  

Laura represents the clearest all-around starting archetype: fast enough to reposition easily, durable enough to recover mistakes, and naturally compatible with multiple build directions.

Her intended early offense combines:
- pulse weapon pressure  
- light rockets  
- clean direct engagement tools  

She should feel immediately readable for new players while still scaling well into advanced synergy builds.

**Strengths**
- Strong baseline adaptability  
- High movement responsiveness  
- Smooth transition into many upgrade paths  

**Weaknesses**
- No naturally dominant specialization  
- Requires smart scaling later against stronger faction pressure  

**Starting Stats**
- **Perception:** High  
- **Charisma:** Medium–High  
- **Ingenuity:** Low

### Roman

**Role Identity:** Drone controller, disruption specialist, indirect pressure  
**Default Ship:** Drone Boat  
**Build Direction:** System interference plus swarm damage  

Roman begins weaker in direct confrontation but gains strength through battlefield manipulation. His intended style rewards disabling enemy advantages while autonomous systems do the damage.

His natural build leans toward:
- drones  
- electronic warfare  
- enemy disruption  
- control effects  

He should feel strongest when the player understands threat priority and timing.

**Strengths**
- Excellent scaling through indirect systems  
- Strong faction-specific counterplay potential  
- High synergy ceiling  

**Weaknesses**
- Lower immediate direct damage  
- Vulnerable when swarm control breaks down  

**Starting Stats**
- **Perception:** Medium  
- **Charisma:** Low  
- **Ingenuity:** High

### The Captain

**Role Identity:** Command pilot, balanced pressure, disciplined versatility  
**Default Ship:** Corvette  
**Build Direction:** Reliable mixed offense with tactical consistency  

The Captain is designed as a highly stable all-around pilot with fewer extremes than other specialists.

His intended starting offense combines:
- lasers  
- missiles  
- balanced engagement ranges  

He should reward disciplined positioning and efficient adaptation to faction pressure.

**Strengths**
- Few major weaknesses  
- Stable performance across factions  
- Strong reliability in difficult runs  

**Weaknesses**
- Lower ceiling than extreme specialist builds unless synergies emerge  

**Starting Stats**
- **Perception:** High  
- **Charisma:** Medium  
- **Ingenuity:** Medium  

---

### Cami

**Role Identity:** Burst skirmisher, high-risk precision aggression  
**Default Ship:** Fast strike platform  
**Build Direction:** Extreme burst damage through timing and angle control  

Cami is built around speed, timing, and punishing exposed targets. She should feel dangerous immediately, but unforgiving when misplayed.

Her intended early offense leans toward:
- rail precision strikes  
- burst volleys  
- bomber-style attack windows  

She relies heavily on positioning and timing rather than sustained defense.

**Strengths**
- Extremely high burst damage  
- Exceptional evasion potential  
- Strong reward for skilled piloting  

**Weaknesses**
- Low shield and hull forgiveness  
- Requires secondary scaling for wave clear  

**Starting Stats**
- **Perception:** High  
- **Charisma:** High  
- **Ingenuity:** Low  

---

### Ghost

**Role Identity:** Meta-build disruptor, economy specialist, high variance  
**Default Ship:** Undetermined / adaptive platform  
**Build Direction:** Weak baseline, accelerated scaling through economy and upgrades  

Ghost is intended as a later unlock that bends normal build rules.

Rather than excelling immediately, Ghost begins weaker but gains power through:
- increased nanobot acquisition  
- improved upgrade efficiency  
- small flat enhancement to upgrade output  

This pilot is designed to reward advanced players who understand long-term scaling.

**Strengths**
- Exceptional late-run growth potential  
- High build experimentation value  
- Can amplify unusual upgrade combinations  

**Weaknesses**
- Weak early survival baseline  
- Requires strong run decisions immediately  

**Starting Stats**
- **Perception:** High  
- **Charisma:** Low  
- **Ingenuity:** High  

## Character Design Principle

Each pilot should feel meaningfully different within the first minutes of a run:
- movement profile  
- opening pressure  
- dialogue tone  
- upgrade temptation  
- emotional pacing  

The player should quickly feel that choosing a pilot changes both **combat identity** and **narrative energy**.

Intended build paths should guide players naturally, but never fully contain them. Strong systems should allow players to discover:
- off-path builds  
- unexpected synergies  
- faction-specific counter-builds  
- rare overpowered combinations  

A pilot should suggest a direction, not impose one.
