## Lore (early narrative foundation)
A small civilian mineral-harvesting station survives on the edge of a dangerous sector, held together by ingenuity, routine, and a family that has learned how to endure isolation.

At the center is Trey, a former military pilot turned engineer, who now operates an aging but capable industrial rig while raising his two children. The station is worn, patched, and imperfect, but dependable — much like Trey himself. What began as survival slowly became home.

His daughter Laura is bright, charming, and instinctively capable. She learns quickly, absorbs everything around her, and already shows unusual aptitude in piloting and systems work.

His son Roman is quieter, endlessly curious, mechanically gifted, and deeply drawn to automation, drones, and systems logic. He constantly reworks station equipment, often improving things no one asked him to touch.

Some time earlier, a stranded military officer known simply as The Captain arrived seeking temporary refuge after losing contact with sector command. Temporary became permanent enough that he now functions as part of the household: managing communications, logistics, security, and station readiness while waiting for word from whatever remains of his former command. He has become an older-brother figure to the children and a trusted equal to Trey.

Later, Cami, a mercenary fleeing a dangerous rival, was reluctantly taken in after arriving under hostile circumstances. The Captain distrusted her first, but over time she proved useful, disciplined, and unexpectedly gentle with the children. Laura idolizes her. Trey values how naturally she stepped into the rhythms of station life. The Captain, despite himself, became attached.

Cami also introduced the station to her personally cultivated onboard AI: Aggy (from Artificial General Intelligence). Originally a private adaptive system, Aggy gradually replaced the station’s primitive control software and became embedded in daily life. Aggy manages systems, assists repairs, runs simulations, teaches, entertains, and answers endless questions — especially Roman’s. Under Aggy’s guidance, Laura became a stronger pilot and mechanic, while Roman pushed his drone algorithms far beyond hobbyist quality.

For a time, the station became something rare in deep space: stable.

Then, without warning, sensors lit across every channel.

A swarm of unidentified objects entered the sector and surrounded the station before a full defensive response could form. Shields failed under concentrated pressure. Boarding breaches followed.

The crew fought back, but the attackers were coordinated, overwhelming, and focused. Before withdrawal, they captured one member of the station crew and detonated charges that left most of the station devastated.

Only two crew members remain.

One stays behind to stabilize life support, maintain communications, and keep the station alive.

The other launches immediately in pursuit.

Which crew member is taken, which remains behind, and who becomes the pursuing pilot depends on the character selected by the player.

That choice determines not only starting strengths and ship identity, but also the emotional perspective of the run: who is being rescued, who is speaking over comms, and why the pursuit matters.
